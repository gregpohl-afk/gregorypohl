BIO

Twenty-five years in pharmaceuticals. Ten of those in radiopharmaceuticals, which — and I say this with genuine affection — is an industry that will absolutely humble you if you let your guard down for thirty seconds.

And it’s a good thing I don’t.

Turns out I’m very good at looking at where you are, where you think you’re going, and spotting the thing that’s going to make the next eighteen months a lot harder than you’d planned. I’m also good at seeing the opportunity you’ve walked past three times because the most plausible solution was right in front of you. That instinct isn’t purely technical, by the way. It’s psychology. Human dynamics. The way narrative shapes decisions before anyone realizes it’s happening.

When I find what I’m looking for I won’t dress it up in a slide deck. I won’t tell you what you want to hear. I will, however, tell you exactly what’s happening — which, in my experience, is what people actually need even when they’re not entirely sure they want it.
